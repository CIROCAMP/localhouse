# The Story Behind Our Famous Lobster Eggs Benedict

Every great restaurant has a signature dish—the one that defines the establishment, that keeps customers coming back, that becomes synonymous with the restaurant's name. For The Local House, that dish is the Lobster Eggs Benedict.

At $28 per plate, it's not the cheapest brunch option in Miami Beach. But it's the one that's made us legendary. Over 2,500 five-star reviews mention this single dish. Food bloggers feature it. Locals recommend it to visitors. It's the reason people search "best brunch in Miami Beach" and end up at our door.

But how did a simple dish—eggs, hollandaise, and bread—become so iconic? That's a story worth telling.

## The Beginning: Italian Hospitality Meets Coastal Cuisine

The Local House was founded by an Italian entrepreneur with a vision: create a restaurant that combined authentic Italian hospitality with Miami Beach's vibrant coastal cuisine. The location—400 Ocean Drive in South of Fifth—was perfect. The oceanfront setting, Art Deco architecture, and exclusive neighborhood provided the ideal backdrop.

But a great location doesn't make a great restaurant. You need a concept, a vision, and most importantly, food that tells a story.

The founder spent months developing the menu, drawing inspiration from three sources: traditional Italian cuisine, fresh local ingredients available in Miami, and the brunch culture that was emerging in Miami Beach. The goal was simple: create dishes that felt authentic, tasted exceptional, and reflected the neighborhood's sophisticated character.

## The Inspiration: A Classic Reimagined

Eggs Benedict is a classic brunch dish with roots in 19th-century New York. The traditional version features poached eggs, Canadian bacon, and hollandaise sauce on an English muffin. It's elegant, balanced, and timeless.

But The Local House wanted to create something more. Something that reflected Miami's coastal identity. Something that would become legendary.

The answer was obvious: lobster.

Fresh Maine lobster is a symbol of quality, luxury, and coastal excellence. By replacing the Canadian bacon with premium lobster meat, the dish became elevated without losing its classic structure. The poached eggs and hollandaise remained—they're essential to the dish's success—but now they were complemented by sweet, tender lobster.

## The Execution: Perfecting Every Element

Creating a signature dish isn't just about choosing premium ingredients. It's about executing each component perfectly and ensuring they work together harmoniously.

### The Lobster

The Local House sources fresh Maine lobster, ensuring the highest quality. The lobster meat is carefully removed from the shell, cleaned, and lightly warmed before plating. The goal is to preserve the lobster's delicate flavor and tender texture without overcooking it.

### The Poached Eggs

Poaching eggs perfectly is an art form. The eggs must be cooked just enough so the whites are set while the yolk remains runny. This requires precise temperature control and timing. At The Local House, the kitchen staff poaches eggs to order, ensuring every plate meets the restaurant's exacting standards.

### The Hollandaise Sauce

Hollandaise is a temperamental sauce made from egg yolks, butter, and lemon juice. It requires constant attention and precise technique. Too much heat and it breaks. Too little and it's too thick. The Local House makes hollandaise fresh daily, ensuring it has the perfect consistency and flavor—rich, creamy, and balanced with just the right amount of acidity from the lemon juice.

### The English Muffin

The foundation of any great Eggs Benedict is quality bread. The Local House uses a toasted English muffin that's sturdy enough to support the eggs and sauce without falling apart, yet tender enough to complement the other elements.

## Why It Works: The Perfect Balance

The Lobster Eggs Benedict succeeds because each element serves a purpose and complements the others:

**The lobster** provides sweetness and luxury, elevating the dish beyond a standard brunch item.

**The poached eggs** provide richness and a runny yolk that creates sauce when broken into.

**The hollandaise** ties everything together with its creamy, buttery richness and subtle lemon brightness.

**The English muffin** provides structure and a neutral base that doesn't compete with the other flavors.

**The Ocean Drive location** and oceanfront views provide the perfect atmosphere for enjoying this special dish.

## The Impact: From Menu Item to Legend

When The Local House opened in 2012, the Lobster Eggs Benedict was one of many brunch options. But it quickly became the standout. Customers raved about it. Food bloggers featured it. Local media highlighted it.

Within a few years, it had become legendary. People specifically came to The Local House to try the Lobster Eggs Benedict. It appeared on "best of" lists for Miami Beach brunch. It became the dish that defined the restaurant.

Today, over a decade later, the Lobster Eggs Benedict remains the most popular brunch item. It's the dish that puts The Local House on the map for new visitors. It's the reason locals keep coming back.
